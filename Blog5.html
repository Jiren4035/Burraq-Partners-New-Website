A Concise Guide About ESG Reporting Requirements in 2025

In today’s rapidly evolving business landscape, environmental, social, and governance (ESG) considerations are no longer optional. Organizations worldwide are aligning with ESG reporting requirements to meet regulatory demands, stakeholder expectations, and global sustainability goals.

What Are ESG Reporting Requirements?

ESG reporting requirements refer to the frameworks, regulations, and standards businesses must adhere to when disclosing their environmental, social, and governance performance. These requirements aim to provide transparency, ensuring stakeholders can assess a company’s sustainability initiatives and their alignment with broader goals.

The Growing Importance of ESG Reporting in Saudi Arabia

Regulatory Landscape

Saudi Arabia has aligned its economic diversification goals under Vision 2030, integrating sustainability into its core objectives. Key ESG initiatives in the region include the Green Saudi Initiative, focused on reducing carbon emissions by 278 million tons annually by 2030, and the Saudi Exchange ESG Disclosure Guidelines.

Investor Focus on ESG

Globally, ESG assets under management (AUM) are expected to exceed $50 trillion by 2025. Saudi Arabia’s Public Investment Fund (PIF) has committed to integrating ESG criteria into its investment decisions, driving demand for accurate and transparent ESG reporting.

Technological Advances Enhancing ESG Reporting Processes

Technological innovations are reshaping how businesses meet ESG reporting requirements. Advanced tools, including artificial intelligence (AI) and blockchain, are providing organizations with the means to manage data more efficiently. In 2024, approximately 30% of global companies adopted AI-driven ESG reporting tools, a number expected to rise to 45% by 2025.

The Role of ESG in Saudi Arabia’s Renewable Energy Sector

In 2024, renewable energy projects accounted for 30% of the country’s total energy mix, with a target to reach 50% by 2030. Key initiatives, such as the NEOM Green Hydrogen Project, align with international ESG frameworks like the GRI and TCFD, ensuring transparent environmental reporting.

The Economic Impact of ESG Compliance in Saudi Arabia

Companies adhering to ESG reporting requirements are more likely to attract foreign investments. In 2024, ESG-compliant businesses in Saudi Arabia reported a 20% increase in foreign direct investment (FDI). By 2025, FDI inflows into ESG-aligned projects are expected to surpass $50 billion.

Key ESG Reporting Frameworks

  • GRI (Global Reporting Initiative): Comprehensive disclosures across ESG metrics. 80% of Saudi companies using GRI report improved trust.
  • SASB (Sustainability Accounting Standards Board): Industry-specific disclosures relevant to investors.
  • TCFD (Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures): Focuses on climate risks. Target compliance of 70% for Saudi listed companies by 2025.
  • ISSB (International Sustainability Standards Board): Aiming to unify global ESG standards.

Steps to Ensure Compliance with ESG Reporting Requirements

1. Identify Applicable Frameworks: Assess which frameworks align with your industry.

2. Conduct a Materiality Assessment: Identify ESG issues relevant to your stakeholders.

3. Set Measurable Goals: Align reporting with objectives like carbon reduction.

4. Leverage Technology: Use AI-powered solutions to improve data accuracy.

5. Engage Stakeholders: Collaborate with regulators and employees.
